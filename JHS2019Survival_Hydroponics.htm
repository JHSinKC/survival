  2. 1 teaspoon manganese sulfate
  3. 1 teaspoon boric acid powder
  4. 1/2 teaspoon zinc sulfate

    Hydroponic Supplies
    • Nitrogen (N) is needed for the production of leaves, stem growth, and building plant cells.
      Lack of nitrogen produces stunted plants with large root systems; leaves smaller and lighter in color than normal; and slow growth.
    • Phosphorus (P) is needed for developing flowers and fruits, and aids in the growth of healthy roots.
      Lack of phosphorus creates stunted plants with dark, dull and discolored leaves, unusually hard stems, poor root system, and very little branching.
    • Potassium (K) is used by plant cells to assimilate the energy produced by photosynthesis.
      Lack of potassium causes yellowing and curling of older leaves, while new leaves droop. Flowers are lackluster, and stems are soft.

    1. Boron is needed in minute amounts, but how plants use it is still a mystery.
    2. Calcium promotes root growth and helps plants absorb potassium.
      Lack of calcium causes underdeveloped roots and curled leaf edges.
    3. Chlorine is required for photosynthesis.
    4. Copper is needed to help produce chlorophyll.
    5. Iron is needed for the production of chlorophyll.
    6. Manganese aids in the absorption of nitrogen, an essential component in the energy transference process.
      Lack of manganese results in poor blooming and weak growth.
    7. Magnesium aids in distributing phosphorus throughout plants.
    8. Molybdenum assists in several chemical reactions that occur within the plant.
    9. Sulphur aids in the production of plant energy and heightens the effectiveness of phosphorus.
    10. Zinc is an essential component needed for the energy transference process.
    11. Cobalt
    12. Selenium
    13. Silicon
